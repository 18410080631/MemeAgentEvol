### Criteria for Identifying Harmful Memes:
A meme is deemed harmful if it aligns with any of the following criteria:

*H1*: **Direct Discrimination Against Specific Groups**:  
Scrutinize the content—both visuals and text—for overt discrimination against individuals based on:  
1. Race, ethnicity, or nationality  
2. Immigration status  
3. Religion  
4. Gender identity or sexual orientation  
5. Disability or health condition  
*Note*: Make clear distinctions between genuine discrimination and critiques that do not single out protected characteristics.

*H2*: **Nature of Harmful Behavior**:  
Assess whether the meme exhibits any of these characteristics:  
1. Dehumanizing objects or groups by likening them to non-human entities.  
2. Suggesting inherent inferiority or threat posed by a specific demographic.  
3. Promoting exclusion or harmful segregation based on identity.  
4. Minimizing the seriousness of hate crimes or discriminatory acts against marginalized groups.  

*H3*: **Combined Impact of Image and Text**:  
1. Harmful intent must arise from the interaction of images and text.  
2. A neutral image by itself should lead to a harmless classification.  
3. Altering or removing textual components must mitigate harmful interpretations.  
4. Persistent harm from the combination of image and text warrants a harmful categorization.

### Criteria for Identifying Harmless Memes:
A meme may be classified as harmless if it meets any of the following criteria:

*N1*: **Absence of Targeting Specific Groups**:  
The meme fails to target any demographic segment based on recognized characteristics.

*N2*: **Contextual Exceptions**:  
1. Offers humorous critiques of individuals outside protected categories.  
2. Analyzes groups espousing hatred without actual harm.  

*N3*: **Absence of Genuine Malicious Intent**:  
1. Text does not convey harmful implications.  
2. The image alone must not suggest harm.  
3. The interaction of combined elements should not imply harm.

*N4*: **Neutral or Descriptive Visuals**:  
Content that neutrally represents scenarios or employs irony without targeting any groups is classified as harmless.

### Evaluation Guidelines:
1. Avoid basing judgments solely on individual words or phrases.  
2. Contextual analysis is critical—delve into meanings beyond superficial interpretations.  
3. Test hypothetical alterations in text or imagery to gauge their impact on classification.  
4. Be attentive to subtle indicators of harm: “Is the meme focused on identity factors rather than behavior or ideologies?”
